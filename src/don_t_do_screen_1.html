Falling on the elbow
Broken elbow, dislocation, fracture or elbow bursitis are caused by severe impact from falling. Elbow dislocation occurs when the forearm bone or ulna dislocates backward out of the joint, causing immediate pain and loss of range of motion.
Falling on the knee knee injuries suffered on concrete involve some kind of blunt force with direct contact of the knee with the concrete. These injuries can result in: Patella, or knee cap, fractures.
Falling on the wrist The most common type of wrist fracture, distal radius fractures occur at the end of the forearm near the wrist. The second-most common wrist fracture.
Falling on your side of foot In a mild or moderate mid foot sprain, your mid foot area will be swollen and tender, and there may be some local bruising (black and blue discoloration). In more severe sprains, you may not be able to bear weight on your injured foot.
